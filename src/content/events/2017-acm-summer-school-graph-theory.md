## 🎓 About

Graph theory is a classical area of research in combinatorial mathematics that studies mathematical structures used to model pairwise relations between objects. It finds applications across a wide range of domains including computational biology, computational neuroscience, communication network design, VLSI design, traffic optimisation, route planning, computer vision, and network visualisation.

This three-week summer school provides a forum for learning and discussing several foundational aspects of **structural graph theory**, interleaved with perspectives from **algorithmic graph theory**. An important emphasis of the curriculum is on the observation that structural results are often exploited to design efficient algorithms. The program features lectures combined with laboratory sessions where participants implement algorithms, learn benchmarking, and work with real-world datasets.

The school was organized by **Prof. Anirban Dasgupta** and **Prof. Neeldhara Misra** at IIT Gandhinagar, in partnership with **ACM India**, with support from the **Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)**.

## 📚 Lecture Series Overview

### Structural Graph Theory (GT)
Graphs and basic definitions; matching; covering and packing; Erdős–Pósa Theorem; connectivity; Menger's Theorem; Mader's Theorem; flows and the Max-Flow Min-Cut Theorem; results from extremal graph theory including Turán's Theorem.

### Elementary Graph Algorithms (GA)
Traversal algorithms (BFS, DFS) and their applications; minimum spanning trees; shortest paths (Dijkstra, Bellman–Ford); maximum matching (Hopcroft–Karp); network flows and applications.

### Graph Decompositions (GD)
k-trees; tree decompositions; pathwidth and treewidth; cops and robber games on graphs; brambles; rank-width and other width parameters.

### Parameterized & Approximation Algorithms (PA)
Vertex cover — approximation and FPT algorithms; kernelization; feedback vertex set via iterative compression; dynamic programming over tree decompositions; color coding; LP-based kernels.

### Special Graph Classes (SGC)
Planar graphs and the Five Color Theorem; Kuratowski's Theorem; chordal graphs and perfect elimination orderings; geometric graphs; the Perfect Graph Theorem.

### Dynamic & Approximation Algorithms (DA)
Introduction to approximation algorithms; 2-approximation for TSP and vertex cover; minimum degree spanning trees; edge coloring; multiway cut (LP relaxation and randomized rounding); hardness of approximation.
