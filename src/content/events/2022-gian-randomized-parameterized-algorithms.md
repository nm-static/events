## Randomized Methods for Approximation and Parameterized Algorithms

**GIAN Course 191014K02 — December 5–9, 2022**

A comprehensive 5-day course covering randomized algorithmic techniques for approximation algorithms, parameterized algorithms, and derandomization methods.

---

Many important computational problems are NP-complete, making it unlikely that they can be solved optimally in polynomial time. Two major approaches to deal with NP-completeness are approximation algorithms and parameterized algorithms. This course covers how randomization leads to approaches that are faster and easier to implement, through problems such as Longest Path, Minimum Cut, Maximum Cut, Clustering, Vertex Cover, Feedback Vertex Set, and Closest String.
