## Computing Research in Curricula: Knowledge, Education & Training

### How to do Computing Education Research

A workshop for CSEd researchers and practitioners (and others) at [IIT Gandhinagar](https://iitgn.ac.in/).

**3rd February 2024 (Saturday) — 5th February 2024 (Monday)**

---

You likely know what it means to study computing. But what does it mean to study computing *education*? What kinds of questions do we ask, and how do we go about answering them?

Join us for the CRiCKET workshop to have a hands-on experience navigating the current and future landscape of CS Education, with a particular emphasis on how *research* (CER: Computing Education Research) in this area works.

Though ostensibly about computing education, what we're really going to study is *how to learn things about humans*. Obviously, education is about humans. But several other branches of computing largely or partly do, or could, work with humans too. Obvious candidates are areas like HCI or usable security; less obvious might be areas like formal methods and programming languages; even systems and algorithms have human components. So anyone who is interested in learning about how to learn things about humans will find useful content here.

The format will not be slides-and-lectures. There will be virtually no slides, and there will be very little lecturing. Rather, we will do lots of hands-on activities, and we will have several conversations. Most of the learning will take place bottom-up.

<div class="callout callout-important">
<div class="callout-title">Target Audience</div>
<div class="callout-body">

The workshop is designed for people who are interested in the methods of learning about humans. You could be a graduate student, postdoc, or faculty member, or a person who works in industry (e.g., EdTech). Either way, you should have a real interest in understanding the methods of working with humans, whether in computing education or some other field.

## Topics

Here are the things we want you to learn to think about in computing education research:

- What kinds of areas does CER cover?
- What kinds of questions does CER ask?
- What kinds of study designs do we see?
- What kinds of analysis methods are used?
- How (and where) are findings presented?

## FAQ

<div class="callout callout-question">
<div class="callout-title">Who is the target audience for this workshop?</div>
<div class="callout-body">

The workshop is designed for people who are interested in the methods of learning about humans. You could be a graduate student, postdoc, or faculty member, or work in industry (e.g., EdTech).

</div>
</div>

<div class="callout callout-question">
<div class="callout-title">Are there any technical pre-requisites?</div>
<div class="callout-body">

There are no explicit pre-requisites, although some experience with the instructional aspects of computing classes will be invaluable for those focused on CER.

</div>
</div>

<div class="callout callout-question">
<div class="callout-title">Can I expect to "get a paper" out of this?</div>
<div class="callout-body">

The workshop is intended to train participants in the methods of learning things about humans. You will leave with your head buzzing with ideas for questions to ask, and will have the tools to follow through on them end-to-end. However, this is not to be thought of as a shortcut to a publication.

</div>
</div>

<div class="callout callout-question">
<div class="callout-title">Will the sessions be open for remote participation and/or recorded?</div>
<div class="callout-body">

No. The goal is to have a high-quality intensive, in-person, hands-on event.

</div>
